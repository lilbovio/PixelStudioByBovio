# Pixel by Bovio — Brand Guidelines

> **Status:** Pre-implementation specification. This document governs every visual and verbal expression of the brand.

---

## 1. Brand Identity

**Studio Name:** Pixel by Bovio
**Tagline:** Digital Design Studio
**Secondary Statement:** We design and build modern websites that help businesses earn trust, attract customers and grow.
**Location:** Guadalajara, Mexico
**Primary Channel:** WhatsApp

---

## 2. Brand Positioning

Pixel by Bovio is not selling websites.

It is selling **growth**. The website is only the tool. The real product is:
- Trust
- Credibility
- Customer attraction
- Business growth

Every piece of brand communication translates technical work into business value.

| Instead of | Say |
|---|---|
| "We build responsive websites" | "Your customers can reach you from any device." |
| "We optimize SEO" | "Help more people discover your business online." |
| "We write clean code" | "Your website loads fast because your customers' time matters." |
| "We do UI design" | "Your website will reflect the quality of your business." |

## 5. Voice and Tone

### Personality in Writing

The copy voice is: **calm, confident, intelligent, direct.**

Imagine a senior advisor who has seen it all — not an excited salesperson, not a textbook, not a friend. Someone who understands business and communicates with quiet authority.

### Core Writing Rules

1. **Short sentences.** Break long sentences into two.
2. **Short paragraphs.** Maximum four lines.
3. **Never justify text.** Left-aligned always.
4. **No corporate clichés.** ("passionate," "innovative," "synergy," "leverage")
5. **No AI-sounding writing.** Avoid: "It's important to note that…", "In today's digital landscape…", "At the end of the day…"
6. **No fake urgency.** No countdown timers. No "Limited spots available."
7. **No exaggerated superlatives.** No "best," "world-class" (unless earned).
8. **No first-person boasting.** Focus on what the client gains.
9. **Outcomes over features.** Always translate tech into business value.
10. **Accessible vocabulary.** Write for business owners, not developers.

### Tone Calibration by Section

| Section | Tone |
|---|---|
| Hero | Bold. Direct. Confident. High impact. |
| Problem | Empathetic. Relatable. Non-judgmental. |
| Solution | Clear. Reassuring. Practical. |
| Services | Professional. Outcome-focused. Brief. |
| Work | Confident. Understated. Let the work speak. |
| Process | Structured. Transparent. Trust-building. |
| Why Us | Direct. Evidence-based. Client-focused. |
| FAQ | Warm. Honest. Helpful. Plain language. |
| Final CTA | Warm invitation. Calm urgency. Low friction. |
| Footer | Minimal. Clean. Factual. |

## 6. Headline Writing Style

Headlines should be short, confident, and memorable. They speak directly to what the client wants.

### Examples of On-Brand Headlines
```
Websites designed to grow your business.
Design that builds trust.
Digital experiences that turn visitors into customers.
Modern websites that customers remember.
Built for ambitious businesses.
Your business deserves to be seen.
Websites that earn trust.
```

### Examples of Off-Brand Headlines
```
We are passionate about creating amazing digital experiences! ✗
Transforming your online presence with cutting-edge solutions ✗
We build responsive, SEO-optimized, conversion-focused websites ✗
Your one-stop-shop for all digital needs ✗
```

## 7. Prohibited Language

Never use these words or phrases in any context:

- "Passionate" / "We are passionate"
- "We love coding"
- "Our mission is to..."
- "World-class" (unless backed by evidence)
- "Revolutionary" / "Innovative" / "Cutting-edge"
- "Best-in-class"
- "Synergy" / "Leverage" / "Streamline"
- "One-stop-shop"
- "Your vision, our mission"
- "We go above and beyond"
- "Take your business to the next level"

## 8. Color Identity

### Primary Palette

| Role | Hex | Usage |
|---|---|---|
| Background | `#FFFFFF` | Primary surfaces |
| Background Alt | `#F9FAFB` | Section alternation |
| Text Primary | `#0F0F0F` | Headlines |
| Text Secondary | `#374151` | Body copy |
| Text Muted | `#6B7280` | Supporting text |
| Button Primary | `#111827` | CTA buttons |
| Accent | `#1A56DB` | Links, focus, emphasis |

### Accent Color Restraint

One accent color only: **Deep Blue `#1A56DB`**.

It appears sparingly — hyperlinks, focus rings, emphasis highlights. Never used for background fills, gradients, or decoration.

### Prohibited Colors
- Purple (any shade)
- Bright green
- Orange
- Rainbow gradients
- Neon or high-saturation palettes
- Gaming-aesthetic dark blues with neon accents

---

## 9. Typography Identity

**Font:** Geist  
**Fallback:** system-ui, sans-serif

One font. Weight variation creates all hierarchy. This constraint communicates discipline and restraint — qualities the brand stands for.

### Headline Style
- Short, typically 4–7 words
- Negative letter-spacing on large sizes (tighter tracking = more premium)
- Heavy weight (700–800) for impact
- Mostly left-aligned

### Body Copy Style
- 16–18px
- Line height 1.65–1.7 (generous — easier to read)
- Short paragraphs (2–4 sentences)
- Never justified

---

## 10. Iconography

**Library:** Lucide React (outline/line style)

Rules:
- One icon family across the entire site
- Consistent stroke width (1.5–2px)
- Consistent sizing per context:
  - Small: 16px
  - Medium: 20px
  - Large: 24px
  - Feature icons: 32px
- Always monochrome (never colored icons)
- Never decorative emoji
- Never filled icon sets
- Icons support content — they never replace it

---

## 11. Imagery Guidelines

**No generic stock photos.** They communicate inauthenticity.

**Use instead:**
- Beautiful UI/website mockups in browser frames
- High-quality device mockups (phone, laptop, tablet)
- Studio-inspired abstract compositions
- Minimal professional photography (if used)
- Project preview screenshots
- Abstract premium visuals (dark, minimal, architectural)

**Image treatment:**
- Slight rounded corners (24px)
- Subtle shadow at rest
- Never stretch or distort images
- Maintain consistent aspect ratios
- Prefer WebP/AVIF formats
- All images have descriptive alt text

## 14. WhatsApp as Primary Channel

WhatsApp is the conversion mechanism. It must feel intentional, not like an afterthought.

**Prefilled message:**
```
Hello Pixel by Bovio,

I found your website and I'm interested in discussing a project for my business.

I would like to receive more information.

Thank you.
```

This message is friendly, non-committal, and easy to send. It removes all friction from starting a conversation.

WhatsApp appears in:
1. Navigation CTA button ("Start a Project")
2. Hero section primary CTA
3. Floating sticky button (after 300px scroll)
4. Final CTA section
5. Footer
6. Contact page

**Rule:** Never require a form before allowing WhatsApp contact. Reduce friction at every point.

## 15. The "If Their Website Looks This Good" Standard

Every design decision should make a business owner think:

> "If their own website looks this good, I want them to build mine."

This is the ultimate brand quality test. Before any page is considered complete, apply this standard.
